DO YOU KNOW?

As the COVID-19 pandemic rages on, safe and effective vaccines were developed and produced quickly.
Vast majority of it were administered in high-income countries. According to the United Nations Development Program, 1 in 10 people have received at least one dose in low- to middle-income countries in contrast to high income countries' 1 to 1 ratio.

In other words, the virus is moving faster than the global distribution of vaccines.
This leaves the low- to middle-income countries vulnerable to the virus and its mutations.

The World Health Organization says there are enough vaccine doses to be distributed globally. However, to cover the cost of vaccinating 70% of the population, low-income countries have to increase their health care spending by 56.6% whereas high-income countries needed only to increase it by 0.8%.

COVAX Facility helps these countries cope with vaccine inequity by purchasing vaccines from the manufacturers and donating them to countries that need them.
